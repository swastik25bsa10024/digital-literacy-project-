## Case Study – UPI Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and new users of digital payment apps. In this type of fraud, scammers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent payment requests.

Typically, the fraud begins when a victim receives a phone call, message, or link from an unknown person pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or seller. The scammer may ask the victim to click on a link or scan a QR code, claiming that it will help them receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment and loses funds from their bank account.

Students and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment security are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online transactions.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful handling of digital payment systems to prevent such frauds.
